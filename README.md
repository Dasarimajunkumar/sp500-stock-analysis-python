# sp500-stock-analysis-python
“This project analyzes S&amp;P 500 stock prices (2014–2017) to understand stock behavior, trends, and trading profitability. Using Python, Pandas, NumPy, Matplotlib, and Seaborn, it performs exploratory data analysis and visualizations to identify patterns, correlations, and top-performing companies.”
Stock Price Behavior and Profitability Analysis of S&P 500 Companies (2014–2017)
📌 Project Overview

This project performs Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA) on historical S&P 500 stock price data (2014–2017) to understand stock behavior, market trends, and trading profitability.
Using Python-based data analysis and visualization tools, the dataset was analyzed to identify patterns, correlations, and the most profitable companies during the selected period.

The project focuses on transforming raw financial data into meaningful insights through statistical analysis and visualizations.

🎯 Objectives
Analyze stock price behavior across multiple S&P 500 companies
Identify companies with the highest trading profit (2014–2017)
Study relationships between opening and closing prices
Understand daily return distributions and stock volatility
Visualize trends and correlations within financial data
🗂 Dataset

The dataset contains historical stock market data of S&P 500 companies from 2014 to 2017.

Key Features
Symbol – Company ticker symbol
Date – Trading date
Open – Opening price of the stock
High – Highest price of the stock during the day
Low – Lowest price of the stock during the day
Close – Closing price of the stock
Volume – Number of shares traded

Additional calculated features:

Daily Return
Price Range
Trading Profit (Close – Open)
⚙️ Technologies Used
Python
Pandas – Data manipulation and analysis
NumPy – Numerical computations
Matplotlib – Data visualization
Seaborn – Statistical visualization
📈 Data Analysis & Visualizations

The project includes several visualizations to explore stock market patterns:

📊 Bar Chart – Top 10 stocks by average closing price
🥧 Pie Chart – Top stocks by trading volume share
📦 Box Plot – Distribution of daily returns across years
🔵 Scatter Plot – Relationship between open and close prices
📉 Line Chart – Stock price trends for major companies
📊 Histogram – Distribution of daily returns
🔥 Correlation Heatmap – Relationships between numerical variables
💰 Profit Analysis – Top 5 trading profit companies (2014–2017)
🔍 Key Insights
Certain companies show significantly higher average closing prices compared to others.
There is a strong correlation between open and close prices, indicating consistent market movement.
Daily returns show volatility with several outliers across years.
Some companies generated higher cumulative trading profits during the period analyzed.
